My Top 3 Websites

Facebook
I probably use facebook the most out of any other site right now. It is a great way to stay connected with friends and family. Facebook also hosts a huge amount of great apps. Most of the apps that I use on facebook are games, but there are also many productive apps too.

Area my eye is drawn to: The news feed.

Area my eye is drawn to when I move back away from the screen: The sponsored ads. No, this is not the most important area of the page to me. It probably is the most important area for the company sponsoring the ad and for investors in Facebook though.

My description of the homepage:

  • Active
  • Cluttered
  • Pretty boring color
  • Basic layout
  • Forum-esque

Problem it solves:

  • Disconnecting from friends and family
  • Having too much personal space
  • Too much privacy

Content:
Everything that anyone in the world wants to share with everyone else in the world via pictures, posts, stories, comments, hashtags, etc etc.

Content description:
Facebook can really contain any kind of content. Most of it is fun, silly stuff, but some people post some pretty serious stuff.

It's very easy to find what I am looking for on Facebook. You can use the search bar or the navigation links to get to pretty much anywhere you want right from the homepage. The search bar and the links are available from any page you are on within facebook.

After being on Facebook for a while, I definitely get the feeling that I wasted a lot of time. Generally I get hooked into spending far too much time on there than necessary.

Facebook doesn't sell anything, only the sponsored ads try to sell you stuff.



TheChive
TheChive is one of my favorite web sites because it is very visually attractive. TheChive posts new albums of photos multiple times per day. There are many categories of photo albums on TheChive, some (most) are hilarious, some are funny, some are humerous, and some are sarcastic. TheChive is also an organization that hosts many charities that are making the headlines for raising an astounding amount of funds for individual charities in a very short time frame. Most of the charities are to help specific people who are disabled or became disabled serving our country or in tragic mishaps.

Area my eye is drawn to: The "Most Popular" section at the top.

Area my eye is drawn to when I move back away from the screen: Still the "Most Popular" section at the top. TheChive does have a few ads on it's homepage, but they do not seem to be the focal point whether I am close nor far away from the screen. This tells me something about the site, the organization, and the fine people keeping TheChive true to itself. KCCO!

Visual Description:

  • Dark
  • Cool
  • Modern
  • Visually pleasing
  • Fun
  • Oh, and: Probably the Best Site in the World.

Problem it solves:

  • Boredom
  • Sadness
  • Curiosity

Content:
Awesomeness in the form of pictures, charities, and comments.

Content description:

  • Fun
  • Awesome
  • Funny
  • Silly

It's fairly easy to find what you are looking for at TheChive. There are links to all categories of photo albums as well as a search bar. However, to find a specific photo album, you must either know the exact name of it to search for it or go through lots of albums under the category it falls in.

Once off the homepage, the search bar and the category links are still available at the top, so it is the same level of difficulty.

After being on TheChive for a while, I am always happy that I spent whatever amount of time on there, but at the same time, that I really accomplished nothing at all.

TheChive has a shop called TheChivery. They sell apparel and a lot of knick-knacks. Yes, I have purchased a hoodie and a T-shirt from TheChivery. I purchased them because they are very comfortable, fashionable, and most importantly, they show that I am a proud supporter of TheChive (proceeds from sales go to charities).

Google (and all google apps such as drive, calendar, maps, etc etc)>
Google is one of my favorite websites simply because google basically runs my life at this point. I use google calendars, google maps, google drive, google docs, google+, gmail, gchat, regular ol' google web search, google music, google play, google photos, google translate, google wallet, YouTube, and so on and so on. Basically I can trace any single thing that I do back to Google in one way or another. Google has made it extremely simple to use everything they offer.

Area my eye is drawn to:
Google's homepage is very very VERY simple. There is basically nothing on it except for the Google logo and a search bar. Can't beat the simplicity of it!

Area my eye is drawn to when I move back away from the screen:
Of course, it's still the Google logo and the search bar since that honestly is all that is on their homepage.

Description:

  • Basic
  • Simple
  • Clean
  • Easy
  • Un-cluttered

Problem it solves:

  • Ignorance
  • Unorganization

Content:
Search engine that is extremely easy to use and is full of smartness. Endless amount of applications that help organize everybody's lives. Maps to navigate.

Content description:

  • Serious
  • Everyday life type of stuff
  • But you can search for anything, so it can be silly too.

If you're on Google, you probably don't know exactly what you are looking for, so it's tough to say how easily you'll find it. But MOST Google searches lead you directly to what you were hoping to find. If you're on Google to use one of their apps like calendar, maps, or drive, it's very easy to get there. Just login with your credentials and click the dropdown box of their apps and voila.

The search bar and the dropdown box of apps go with you on whatever page you are on within google.

If I have spent some time on google doing google searches, I would probably be frustrated after a while of not being able to find what I am looking for. But, if I end up spending a lot of time working on my calendar, a google doc, or on gmail, usually I leave with a feeling of accomplishment.

I don't think Google sells anything directly from their website, though I am not sure about that. No, I have not bought any google products yet, but it seems like maybe I should show some support after listing out all of their apps that I use daily!
